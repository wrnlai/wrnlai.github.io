Overview

Ubiquity Press is an academic publisher focusing on open access publication. Their mission is to make quality open access publishing affordable for everyone and create academic tools for research.As one of their very first design interns, Mattin Delavar and I designed their flagship repository product to empower academics and researchers.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

An academic repository that simplifies discovery during the research process.

Current academic repositories are notorious for their cognitive overload and lack of information architecture. We believe that academic search should be one that simplifies search, and one that promotes discoverability of an institutionâ€™s research.

NEEDFINDING

Core Insights

Through an extensive competitive analysis and user interviews with various figures in academia, we learned about various use cases and their perceptions of current repositories.

Based on our research we derived three design goals that Ubiquity Repositories is based on.

i. Communicates ready-to-use experience

A repository's interface should invite the user to engage with the platform and its contributors. It should consistently update its materials to communicate the impact of the work being done at the institution.

ii. Search that feels in control

The repository must create a search process that communicates ease of control and seamlessly integrate with the information architecture of the repository.

iii. Convey impact of authors + work

The repository must present statistics that demonstrate the impact of the authors' work and should serve as a platform for discovering researchers in mutual fields.
