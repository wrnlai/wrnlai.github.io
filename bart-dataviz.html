BART Infographic

Date

September '19 - December '19

Position

Data Visualization Student – Info 190

Background

As frequent users of BART, we all had a mutual understanding of the feelings and sentiments surrounding BART: frustration from delays, poor customer service, and lack of cleanliness. However, our experience only amounted to singular data points in the wide array of the BART experience. We wanted to shed light on the overall performance of BART by analyzing their open-access data set.

Challenge

Through Introduction to Data Visualization, a class taught at UC Berkeley, my team and I created a data infographic to highlight the overall performance trends of BART over the past years.

Skills

  • Data Visualization
  • Visual and Graphic Design
  • Exploratory Data Analysis

My Visualizations

Hero Image

I created this hero image to characterize the growing complaints and negative public perceptions of BART. We thought it’d be fit to create a graphic using complaint data to establish this perception quantitatively. In doing so, this hero graphic acknowledges the negatives (i.e. customer complaints) about BART but also establishes a pivot to argue for the positive progress that BART has made.

Singular Events

Many passengers of BART often draw on their own experiences riding BART to shape their overall perception of the transit system. I created this graphic to draw light to singular occurrences in BART’s history that had high delay rates (30-35% trains arrived on time). I then juxtaposed these graphics to the overall on-time performance from 2015-2017, where 90% of trains arrived on time. These graphics were informed by BART’s on-time data sets, which were then cross referenced with local news sources.

Percent of on-time passengers

This visualization represents the overall on-time performance of BART trains from 2015 to 2017. On average, 90.7% of trains arrived on time from from 2015 to 2017. This visualization was made on Tableau, also using BART’s on-time data sets.

Operating Costs

One of the biggest complaints we had heard was that fare prices were far too expensive. After exploring the budgeting data sets, I created this ripped BART ticket graphic to illustrate the subsidized portion of BART (20%) compared to the 80% paid by passengers.
